This article examines how Nihal Atsız transformed literary history into a national canon that mythologized pre-Islamic Turkic identity and aestheticized a racialized vision of Turkish nationalism.

## Abstract

This article examines Nihal Atsız’s Türk Edebiyatı Tarihi not simply as a work of literary historiography, but as an ideological project of national canon formation. It argues that, while Atsız followed the Turkological trajectory established by Mehmet Fuat Köprülü, he radicalized it by constructing the pre-Islamic past as the purified origin of an essential Turkish identity. Through his periodization of Turkish history, his understanding of language, and his emphasis on national heroes, Atsız turned literature into a medium for producing historical continuity and legitimizing a future-oriented political vision shaped by Turkism and Turanism. The article further demonstrates that Atsız’s literary and historical writings cannot be separated from his political thought, since his canon translated racialized nationalism into compelling aesthetic symbols that continue to inform Turkish nationalist imaginaries.

## Contribution

- Repositions Nihal Atsız within debates on national canon formation.
- Demonstrates how literary historiography functions as an ideological practice of myth-making.
- Explains the role of the national hero in aestheticizing racialized nationalism.
