This article examines how the post-reform university used the University Weeks to position itself as both a scientific institution and a public actor.

## Abstract

This article examines the reorganization of Istanbul University after the 1933 University Reform through the conceptual framework of the Humboldtian higher education model. Focusing on the principles of the **unity of teaching and research**, the **unity of science**, and **Bildung**, it argues that the reform sought to position the university not only as an institution of knowledge transmission and scientific research, but also as a central actor in shaping the social and intellectual life of the Republic. The article analyzes the University Conferences and University Weeks as practical expressions of this new mission, showing how the post-reform university presented itself to the public while also promoting a particular understanding of educated citizenship and elite formation.

## Contribution

- Reconnects the reorganization of Istanbul University with the **Humboldtian model**, *Bildung*, and the unity of teaching and research.
- Analyzes the **University Weeks** as an institutional expression of the new university model.
- Shows how the post-reform university disseminated scientific knowledge across the country in line with state policy and emerged as an active actor in the cultural formation of early Republican Turkey.
